Research & Architecture

Geotechnical hazard assessment through Artificial Intelligence

Project Motivation & Objectives

Landslides represent one of the most destructive natural hazards globally, causing significant loss of life and infrastructure damage. Traditional monitoring relies on manual field surveys and visual interpretation of optical imagery, which is labor-intensive and often limited by cloud cover.

This research platform demonstrates an automated pipeline that leverages Multi-Temporal Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR) combined with state-of-the-art Deep Learning (SegFormer) and Vision-Language Models (VLM). The objective is to provide a rapid, automated, and highly accurate assessment tool for geotechnical engineers and researchers.

Technology Stack

Multi-Temporal InSAR

Phase and Coherence data derived from Sentinel-1 SAR imagery for mm-scale surface deformation mapping.

SegFormer AI

Transformer-based semantic segmentation model optimized for Earth Observation data.

Analysis Engine

Python-based deterministic engine computing geometric, phase, and statistical properties of detected hazards.

Vision-Language Models

Multimodal Qwen2.5-VL model for generating professional engineering reports and interactive Q&A.
